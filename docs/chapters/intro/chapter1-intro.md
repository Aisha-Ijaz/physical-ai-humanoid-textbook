# Introduction to Physical AI

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:
- Define Physical AI and distinguish it from traditional AI approaches
- Understand the key components of Physical AI systems
- Identify applications of Physical AI in real-world scenarios
- Appreciate the interdisciplinary nature of Physical AI

## What is Physical AI?

Physical AI represents a paradigm shift from traditional AI that operates primarily in digital spaces to AI systems that interact with the physical world. Unlike conventional AI that processes text, images, or data, Physical AI integrates computation with physical embodiment, sensorimotor capabilities, and environmental interaction.

### Key Characteristics of Physical AI

- **Embodiment**: Physical form that interacts with the environment
- **Sensorimotor Integration**: Coordinated sensing and action
- **Real-time Processing**: Response to dynamic physical environments
- **Adaptive Behavior**: Learning and adjustment based on physical interactions

## The Interdisciplinary Nature of Physical AI

Physical AI draws from multiple fields:

- **Computer Science**: Algorithms, machine learning, computer vision
- **Mechanical Engineering**: Design of physical structures and actuators
- **Electrical Engineering**: Sensor systems, control circuits
- **Neuroscience**: Understanding biological sensorimotor systems
- **Cognitive Science**: Models of embodied cognition

This interdisciplinary approach is essential for creating AI systems that can effectively interact with the complex physical world.

## Applications of Physical AI

Physical AI finds applications in numerous domains:

- Humanoid robotics
- Autonomous vehicles
- Industrial automation
- Assistive technologies
- Search and rescue robots
